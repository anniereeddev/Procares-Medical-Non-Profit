Understanding EDS

Ehlers-Danlos syndrome (EDS) is a group of genetic connective tissue disorders that affect the body's ability to produce collagen, a crucial protein that provides strength and elasticity to various tissues, including the skin, joints, blood vessels, and organs. There are several types of EDS, each with its own set of symptoms and severity levels.

Living with EDS

Living with EDS can be both challenging and unpredictable. The experiences of individuals with EDS can vary widely, depending on the type and severity of the condition. Symptoms can include chronic pain, joint instability, fatigue, gastrointestinal problems, and cardiovascular concerns.

Resources and Support for EDS

There are several resources available to help individuals with Ehlers-Danlos syndrome (EDS) and their families better understand the condition, manage symptoms, and connect with a supportive community.
