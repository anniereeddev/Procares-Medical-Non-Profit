Meet Ally

Ally has Ehlers-Danlos Syndrome, Hypermobile type, and suffers from related conditions such as Postural Orthostatic Tachycardia Syndrome (POTS), chronic pain, spontaneous intracranial hypotension (SIH), and several abdominal compression syndromes. During her childhood, many pediatric specialists and providers dismissed her symptoms as being psychosomatic. In this video, Ally shares her journey and emphasizes the crucial role knowledgeable physicians played in her healing process.

Cole's Story

True Story: Cole has POTS (Postural Tachycardia Syndrome) and struggles to find help, becoming disabled by it. Pediatric providers kept telling him it was all in his head. But once he found knowledgeable care providers who knew what to do, he found healing.

Dr. Warner is a triple board-certified physician specializing in general and cardiotheracic anesthesiology and interventional pain medicine. He went on to graduate with honors from Ross University School of Medicine, followed by anesthesiology residency at the University of Texas. Lauren Miller

Lauren loves her family, frenchies, and finance. She has a BS in honors finance from the University of Utah. She worked in private equity for a few years before going back to school to earn on MBA from George Washington University. Since graduating from GWU in 2021, she has worked for the Larry H and Gail Mill Family Foundation doing private asset allocation. She has a sister with dysautonomia diseases and she has watched her suffer for the past decade. It has been a family fight and Lauren is passionate in doing what she can to help further the research and doctor education surrounding this disease state. Lauren has a wonderful husband, Josh, two young kids, and two French bulldogs. It's a fun and chaotic household.
